About GrowthRx
Built for people who want to think in businesses.
Most business content is either too shallow (LinkedIn posts with "5 lessons from Tesla") or too dense (700-page annual reports that require a CA to decode). GrowthRx exists in the middle.

We take real company data — from annual reports, RHP filings, and public financials — and turn it into structured, visual, insight-led analysis that anyone with basic business sense can learn from.

Every company page answers the same three questions: How does this business make money? Where does every rupee go? And what's the one thing most people are missing about this company?
What You Get on Every Company Page
💼
Business Model Breakdown
How the company actually makes money — not just what they sell, but the mechanics of each revenue stream and what % it contributes.
📊
Full P&L Waterfall
Revenue → COGS → Gross Margin → every cost head → EBITDA → PBT → PAT. All as % of revenue so you can compare across companies and industries.
🔍
Cost Deep Dive
Not just numbers — each cost head explained in plain language. What's driving it, is it a problem or a feature, and how it compares to competitors.
💡
Unique Insights
3 things about each company that aren't covered in the usual news cycle — things buried in filings, structural advantages, or narrative disconnects worth knowing.
Our Principles
01
Real data, always sourced
Every number comes from public filings — annual reports, SEBI filings, RHP documents. We cite sources. We don't estimate unless clearly marked as such.
02
No financial advice
This is educational content. GrowthRx is not SEBI registered. Nothing here is a recommendation to buy or sell any security. We analyse businesses — not stocks.
03
Plain language, not jargon
If you need a glossary to understand an article, we've failed. We write for people who understand business fundamentals — not for CAs or analysts who already know everything.
04
Free. Forever.
GrowthRx will always be free. No paywall, no subscription. Business intelligence should be accessible to students and founders, not just people who can afford Bloomberg terminals.
